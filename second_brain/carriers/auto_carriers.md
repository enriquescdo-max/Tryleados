# Auto Carrier Appetite Guide — TX Market

## Progressive
- Best for: Most profiles, standard to mid-risk, continuous coverage
- Strengths: Competitive rates, accepts 1-2 prior claims, broad vehicle appetite
- Sweet spot: Fair-good credit, 1-2 claims, vehicles 2010+
- Avoid: 3+ claims, multiple DUIs

## GEICO
- Best for: Excellent/good credit, clean record, newer vehicles
- Strengths: Lowest rates for clean profiles
- Weaknesses: Very strict on credit, lapses, claims
- Sweet spot: 720+ credit, 0 claims, vehicle 2015+
- Avoid: Prior lapses, poor credit, 2+ claims

## Root Insurance
- Best for: Good drivers regardless of credit — telematics-based
- Key differentiator: IGNORES credit score, scores on driving behavior
- Sweet spot: Fair/poor credit but clean driving record — Root WINS here
- Avoid: High mileage 20k+, frequent hard braking

## National General
- Best for: Non-standard — prior lapses, SR-22, multiple claims
- Use when: Progressive and GEICO decline
- Sweet spot: 1-2 lapses, SR-22 required, 2-3 claims

## Bristol West
- Best for: Highest risk, last resort
- Use when: National General also declines
- Sweet spot: Multiple violations, DUI history

## TX Notes
- SR-22 required after DUI, suspension, uninsured accident
- TX minimum: 30/60/25 — always recommend higher
- PIP required unless rejected in writing
- TX has 20%+ uninsured rate — always pitch UM coverage
